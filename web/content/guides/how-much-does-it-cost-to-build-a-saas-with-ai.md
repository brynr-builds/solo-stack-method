Everyone's asking: "How much does it really cost to build a SaaS with AI?" The answers range from "$50/month with AI tools" to "$10,000 minimum for a real product." The gap between theory and practice is wide — so here's the honest math, tool by tool.

Here's what actually goes out the door in the first 90 days for a solo founder building with Claude Code, Lovable, Bolt, or Cursor. (Prices are approximate as of June 2026 — confirm current pricing on each tool's site.)

## The 30-second answer

- **Just starting, want to learn the ropes?** → **$150–$300/month.** Cloudways hosting ($30) + Kit ($15) + ClickFunnels ($97) + Copy.ai ($35) = covers hosting, email, funnel, and AI writing.
- **You're building a business and need it to scale** → **$250–$500/month.** Kinsta hosting ($100) + Kit ($25) + ClickFunnels ($127) + Copy.ai ($55) = faster hosting, bigger email list, better conversion.
- **You're serious and want to optimize** → **$400–$800/month.** Kinsta ($200) + Kit ($45) + ClickFunnels ($297) + Copy.ai ($80) = enterprise hosting, unlimited email, premium funnels.

The catch: these costs are recurring. There's no "pay once and it's done" among these tools.

## Where the money actually goes each month

Four line items cover the core of an AI-built SaaS. Here's how the stack compounds:

**Cloudways** — ~$30/month
Where AI-built apps actually go live. Managed cloud hosting (DigitalOcean under the hood) means you get real server power without becoming a sysadmin.

**Kit (ConvertKit)** — ~$15–$45/month
The email tool we recommend building your list on. Your list is the one asset you own outright — start collecting emails before you think you need to.

**ClickFunnels** — ~$97–$297/month
The funnel builder for people who don't want to become conversion-rate experts. The biggest line item here — add it only when you have something to sell.

**Copy.ai** — ~$35–$80/month
Our AI-writing pick. Drafts the marketing copy, emails, and descriptions you'd otherwise procrastinate on.

## How we priced these picks

We weighted four things that matter to solo builders:

1. **Time-to-first-dollar** — how fast you can go from "I shipped something" to "I made $29."
2. **No-DevOps requirement** — does it need a PhD to configure or just work?
3. **Where the cliffs are** — every SaaS tool gets expensive as you scale; we wanted the gentlest slopes.
4. **Ecosystem compounding** — tools that work better together over time (Kit + ClickFunnels, for example).

## Cloudways - the "where it actually lives" cost

**Best for:** AI-built apps that need to just run without DevOps headaches.

**Pros:** the sweet spot for "I built something with AI and need it to actually be online"; includes managed security, backups, and basic scaling; genuinely good support when you need it.
**Cons:** the dashboard can feel a bit clunky compared to newer hosts; some advanced features require digging through documentation.
**The catch we'd flag:** you choose the underlying cloud (DigitalOcean/Vultr/AWS) at signup — start on the smallest DigitalOcean box and scale the server later; don't pay for capacity you don't have users for.

## Kit (ConvertKit) - the email list foundation

**Best for:** anyone building an owned audience from day one.

**Pros:** the tag-based subscriber model is the right mental model for creators; automations are visual and powerful; the creator ecosystem (commerce, recommendations) genuinely compounds.
**Cons:** pricing climbs steeply with subscriber count (starts generous, then jumps); the visual builder is less "designer-friendly" than newer tools.
**The catch we'd flag:** if you just want a monthly newsletter and never plan to sell anything, Kit is more tool than you need. MailerLite would be cheaper.

## ClickFunnels - the conversion machine

**Best for:** founders who want to turn visitors into buyers without becoming conversion experts.

**Pros:** the widest feature set that actually works (landing pages, order forms, upsells, downsells); genuinely good at what it does; integrates well with Kit.
**Cons:** $97/month feels steep when you're just starting; the interface has aged a bit; can feel restrictive if you want to customize beyond its templates.
**The catch we'd flag:** annual billing discounts are tempting, but don't commit to a year until your funnel has actually converted real visitors — monthly first, annual once it's earning its keep.

## Copy.ai - the AI writing workhorse

**Best for:** founders who write a lot (emails, landing pages, blog posts) and want AI help.

**Pros:** actually decent at writing marketing copy; purpose-built templates beat raw-prompting a chatbot; integrates with most tools out of the box.
**Cons:** outputs can feel generic if you don't feed it your own voice, examples, and specifics.
**The catch we'd flag:** the free tier is enough to find out if it fits your workflow — don't pay until it's already saving you hours.

## Our pick

For a brand new solo founder, we'd start on **Cloudways** and **Kit** to keep costs under $50/month while you figure out what you're building. Once you have something that actually sells, add **ClickFunnels** for the conversion machine. Add **Copy.ai** when you find yourself writing more than you're building.

The most important move isn't picking the perfect tools — it's picking any tools and getting your first product live. The stack is detail; the business is the asset.
