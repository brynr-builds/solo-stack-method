Every solo creator eventually faces this question: **Do I build my course on Teachable or Thinkific?** Both are solid platforms designed for one thing — helping you monetize knowledge. But they go about it differently, which matters when you're just starting out.

We ran both through our usual stack research rubric. Here's the short version, then the detailed breakdown.

## The 30-second answer

- **Just starting, want to set up fast and cheap?** → **Thinkific.** The free tier is genuinely free, onboarding is smooth, and the interface feels native to the experience.
- **You're selling to creators who care about polish and ecosystem?** → **Teachable.** The brand recognition is stronger, and the creator ecosystem (integrations, templates) feels more complete.
- **Pricing matters most and you'll stick with basics?** → **Teachable.** Free tier is more restrictive but comes with real value at each paid tier jump.

There is no universally "best" — there's a best *for where you are and where you're going*. Below is how we'd actually choose.

## How we compared them

We weighted four things a solo builder really cares about in the first 180 days: **time-to-first-lesson** (how fast you can actually sell something), **feature depth** (can you run webinars, community, etc. without another tool), **branded experience** (does it look and feel like *your* thing), and **the upgrade path** (when does the price hurt and what do you get for it).

## Teachable — best for creators who want polish

**Best for:** course creators who want a branded, professional experience and will eventually want integrations.

- **Pros:** cleaner UI that feels like a product, not a platform; solid mobile app experience; strong community features on paid tiers; branding options feel more flexible.
- **Cons:** the free tier caps students at 20 and is very limited (you'll need upgrade sooner); checkout flow can feel less streamlined; the support documentation is good but sometimes hard to navigate.
- **The catch we'd flag:** they position as "all-in-one" but you'll still need email marketing — the platform can replace your learning management system but not your marketing stack.

## Thinkific — best for starters who want simplicity

**Best for:** creators who want to get their first lesson live fast and don't want to think about complex features yet.

- **Pros:** genuinely useful free tier (20 students, no forced watermarks, decent customization); cleaner checkout experience; better export options if you ever need to move; the "themes" feel more like real course sites.
- **Cons:** some advanced features (like community or complex automation) are newer and feel less mature; the "advanced" settings can be buried; mobile experience is good but not as polished as Teachable's.
- **The catch we'd flag:** payment timing can be slower compared to some platforms (30-day hold on Thinkific vs faster on others).

## Our pick

For a solo builder just starting, we'd lean toward **Thinkific** and enjoy the genuinely useful free tier longer. For anyone whose course is already their business and looks matter more, we'd go with **Teachable** for the stronger brand recognition and ecosystem. 

Whichever you choose, the bigger win is just getting your first lesson live. The platform is a detail; the content and audience are the real assets.
