Your email list is the one asset you actually own. Not your followers, not your SEO rankings —
the list. So the tool you build it on matters more than almost anything else in your stack.

We've shipped projects on all three of these. Here's the short version, then the detail.

## The 30-second answer

- **Just starting, want the lowest friction?** → **MailerLite.** Cleanest editor, generous free
  tier, easiest to learn. Start here if email is new to you.
- **You're a creator/founder who will sell digital products?** → **Kit (formerly ConvertKit).**
  Built around creators; tagging and automation are first-class; the ecosystem (commerce,
  recommendations) compounds.
- **You want one tool to do email + landing pages + light CRM/webinars?** → **GetResponse.**
  The most features per dollar, and the most room to grow before you migrate.

There is no universally "best" — there's a best *for where you are*. Below is how we'd actually choose.

## How we compared them

We weighted four things a solo builder feels within the first 90 days: **time-to-first-send**
(how fast you can get a working list), **automation depth** (can it do behavior-based sequences
without a PhD), **deliverability reputation**, and **where the price cliff is** (every email tool
gets expensive as your list grows — the question is *when*).

## MailerLite — start here if you're new

**Best for:** first-timers, newsletters, anyone who wants "send a nice email" without a manual.

- **Pros:** the friendliest editor of the three; a real free tier; clean automations; strong
  deliverability for the price.
- **Cons:** the lightest automation logic of the three — you'll feel the ceiling if you build
  complex behavioral funnels; some advanced blocks are paid-tier only.
- **The catch we'd flag:** approval/onboarding is strict about list quality (a good thing long-term),
  so import clean lists only.

## Kit (ConvertKit) — best for creators who sell

**Best for:** course creators, writers, and founders who'll sell digital products to their list.

- **Pros:** tag-based subscriber model is the right mental model for creators; automations are
  powerful and visual; the creator ecosystem (commerce, Sponsor Network, recommendations) is a
  real growth flywheel you don't get elsewhere.
- **Cons:** the visual builder is less "designer-friendly" than MailerLite (Kit is opinionated that
  plain-text-style emails convert better — they're often right, but you have less control); pricing
  climbs with subscriber count.
- **The catch we'd flag:** if you just want a pretty monthly newsletter and will never sell anything,
  Kit is more tool than you need.

## GetResponse — best all-in-one before you outgrow it

**Best for:** founders who'd otherwise buy three tools — email, landing pages, and a webinar/CRM bolt-on.

- **Pros:** the widest feature set of the three (landing pages, forms, basic CRM, even webinars on
  higher tiers); a genuinely long cookie/relationship if you grow with it; strong automation.
- **Cons:** more surface area means a steeper first hour; the cheapest plan gates the features that
  make it worth choosing over MailerLite.
- **The catch we'd flag:** don't buy GetResponse for *just* email — at that job MailerLite is simpler
  and Kit is better for creators. Buy it when you want the all-in-one.

## Our pick

For a brand-new solo builder, we'd start on **MailerLite** and not look back until the automation
ceiling actually hurts. For anyone whose plan includes *selling to the list* — a course, a template,
a SaaS — we'd start on **Kit** and grow into its ecosystem. Reach for **GetResponse** specifically
when you want to collapse email + landing pages + light CRM into one bill.

Whichever you choose, the move that actually matters is *starting the list today*. The tool is a
detail; the list is the asset.
