# Marketing drafts — agents draft here, **you** post

> The autonomous marketing back office writes drafts into this folder. **The agent never posts to
> any platform** — that's how you avoid Reddit/X/HN bans (automating engagement = suspension in 2026).
> You review, tweak, and post from your own (aged, genuinely-participating) accounts.

## The workflow
1. Run the marketing pipeline (`~/.hermes/pipelines/solo-stack-marketing.sh --dispatch`) — or it runs weekly.
2. Drafts land here as dated markdown: `x-thread-*.md`, `reddit-*.md`, `newsletter-*.md`.
3. You skim each, edit for your voice, and **post it yourself** on a schedule.
4. SEO articles are different — those go through the content pipeline → repo → auto-publish (they're
   governed/audited). Only social/community drafts live here for manual posting.

## Non-negotiable rules (the agents follow these; double-check anyway)
- **FTC affiliate disclosure in any post that mentions a tool we earn from** — in the post, above the
  fold, plain language (see templates below). Penalty is ~$51k/violation; the brand shares liability.
- **Reddit 90/10 rule** — ≤10% self-promotion. Age accounts 2–4 weeks + ~30 substantive comments in a
  sub before any product mention. Never drop a link in 5 subs in a day (instant shadowban).
- **X** — schedule your own posts; never automate follow/like/reply/DM (instant suspension).
- **No identical cross-posting** across subs/accounts (coordinated-inauthentic-behavior flag).

## FTC disclosure templates (copy into posts)
- **In an article / long post:** *"Some links here are affiliate links — if you sign up through them
  I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I only recommend tools I actually use."*
- **X (above the fold, before any link):** *"(affiliate links below — I may earn a commission)"*
- **Reddit comment mentioning a paid tool:** *"Full disclosure: I run solostackmethod.io and some of
  those links are affiliate links."*

## What you post where (from the plan)
- **Reddit:** r/SideProject, r/SaaS, r/indiehackers, r/vibecoding — value first, product last.
- **X:** build-in-public threads (real numbers convert best).
- **Newsletter:** your owned channel — send the weekly issue.
- **Directories/listicles:** submit the drafted entries (CAPTCHAs are yours).
